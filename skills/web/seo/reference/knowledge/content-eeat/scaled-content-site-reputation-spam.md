# Content spam policies — scaled content, site-reputation abuse, doorway, thin affiliate

A cluster of Google spam policies target low-value content at scale; violations draw manual actions/demotion and are the concrete failure states behind "thin/duplicate/doorway/scaled" flags.

## Facts
- **Scaled content abuse**: "when many pages are generated for the primary purpose of manipulating search rankings and not helping users" — covers AI mass-generation, scraping with minimal transformation, and stitching sources without added value. (March 2024 policy; replaced/broadened the old "spammy auto-generated content".) [Google spam-policies; Google core-update-2024-03]
- **Site reputation abuse** (aka "parasite SEO"): third-party content published on a host mainly to exploit the host's established ranking signals. The **Nov 2024 update removed the first-party loophole**: no amount of first-party involvement/oversight (white-label, licensing, partial ownership, sponsored sections) changes the third-party nature or makes it compliant. Enforced by manual actions. [Google spam-policies; site-rep-2024-11]
- **Expired domain abuse**: buying an expired domain and repurposing it (e.g., affiliate/casino/medical content on a former nonprofit or school domain) primarily to inherit ranking signals. [Google spam-policies]
- **Doorway abuse**: pages/sites created to rank for many similar queries that funnel users to one intermediate destination rather than directly to what they wanted. [Google spam-policies]
- **Thin affiliation**: affiliate content whose product descriptions/reviews are copied from the merchant with no original content or added value. **Scraping/duplicate**: republishing others' content with no added value. Both are policy violations, not just "weak content". [Google spam-policies]
- These are the enforceable definitions behind a "thin/duplicate/doorway/scaled" finding — cite the policy, don't invent a word-count threshold.
