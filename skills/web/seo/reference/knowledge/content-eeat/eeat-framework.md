# E-E-A-T — the quality-rater framework Google's core systems approximate

E-E-A-T is not a direct ranking score but the rater rubric Google's automated core signals are built to approximate; Trust is the one member that anchors the other three, and YMYL topics get the strictest scrutiny.

## Facts
- E-E-A-T = Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust. The extra "E" (Experience — first-hand/lived use of a product, a visited place, a lived situation) was added Dec 2022; before that it was E-A-T. [QRG-2025-09]
- **Trust is central**: the QRG states Trust is the most important member — a page low on Trust is Lowest quality regardless of how Experienced/Expert/Authoritative it looks. The other three are supporting notions that feed Trust. [QRG-2025-09]
- E-E-A-T is NOT itself a ranking factor / a number the algorithm computes; it is the human-rater rubric whose judgments Google uses to validate and train the automated core signals. Raters' scores do not directly move any one page. [Google helpful-content; Google reps repeatedly]
- **YMYL** (Your Money or Your Life — health, finance, safety, civic/legal topics where low quality could harm) gets the strictest quality bar. Sept 11 2025 QRG expanded YMYL to explicitly cover elections, voting, public institutions and "trust in society" (the "YMYL Society" bucket renamed "YMYL Government, Civics & Society"). [QRG-2025-09] (confidence: medium — single QRG revision, corroborated by multiple secondary reports)
- Experience matters even without formal credentials: genuine first-hand knowledge can outweigh formal expertise for many topics; for YMYL, formal expertise/credentials still carry the most weight. [QRG-2025-09]
