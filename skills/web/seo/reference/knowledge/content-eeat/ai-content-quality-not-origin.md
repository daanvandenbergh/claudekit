# AI-generated content — judged by quality, not origin

Google does not penalize content for being AI-generated; it evaluates quality and purpose, and only crosses into spam when generation (AI or otherwise) is used to mass-produce low-value pages to manipulate rankings.

## Facts
- Google's stance: content is judged on quality and whether it helps people, **not on how it was produced**. AI-generated content is not banned and can rank/earn high quality ratings when it shows genuine editorial oversight, expertise and added value. [Google gen-ai; Google 2023-02 AI-content blog]
- The line is **behavioral, not tool-based**: "using generative AI tools or other similar tools to generate many pages without adding value for users may violate the spam policy on scaled content abuse." Whether pages are made by AI, humans, or both is irrelevant — the abuse is producing at scale primarily to rank. [Google gen-ai; Google core-update-2024-03]
- Raters flag main content that appears auto/AI-generated with little originality or added value as Lowest quality; AI-*assisted* content with real oversight is not penalized. So AI is a quality-neutral input, gated by E-E-A-T and the spam policies. [QRG-2025-09]
- Google encourages (does not require for ranking) disclosing how automation/AI was used where it aids user trust; for e-commerce, AI-generated images/product data should be labeled with metadata. [Google gen-ai]
- Reject the myth that AI content is auto-penalized; also reject that hand-added JSON-LD or word count rescues thin AI output — see foundations/2026-seo-myths and answer-engines/citeable-patterns.
