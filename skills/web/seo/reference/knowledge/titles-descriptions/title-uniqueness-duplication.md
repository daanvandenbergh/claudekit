# Auditing title uniqueness — missing, empty, and duplicate titles

Missing, empty, or site-wide-duplicated titles are the highest-signal title defects because they directly trigger Google's own rewrite behavior; each page must carry one unique, content-derived title.

## Steps
1. Flag as defects: missing/empty <title>, titles duplicated across many pages, and generic/boilerplate titles — these map directly onto Google's stated replacement triggers (half-empty, micro-boilerplate). [g-title-link]
2. Every page must have exactly one <title>, unique to that page and describing that page's content; duplication across a template is a micro-boilerplate signal Google actively works around. [g-title-link]
3. Fix path: derive a missing title from the page's own H1/prominent content (an additive Tier-A fix); rewriting existing copy is a Tier-B proposal — front-load the distinguishing keyword and keep site branding to a concise suffix. [g-title-link]
4. Do NOT gate pass/fail on an exact character count — see [thresholds](../foundations/thresholds.md) and [2026-seo-myths](../foundations/2026-seo-myths.md); uniqueness and accuracy are the pass criteria, length is guidance. [g-title-link]
